# Start Here

## What Thirsty-101 is

Thirsty-101 is a public interface to my documented mind.

It is a place for anything I choose to preserve: thoughts, memories, questions, experiences, people, references, projects, systems, unfinished fragments, contradictions, patterns, and whatever else becomes worth recording.

It serves me first as external memory and connected thought. It serves the public second by giving other people a path through what I have chosen to expose.

## What it is not

This is not:

- a Project-AI documentation repository;
- a project portfolio with personal notes attached;
- a requirement that every thought become formal or useful;
- a claim that everything recorded here is correct, complete, or permanent;
- a polished timeline that hides uncertainty, contradiction, or changes of mind.

Projects—including Project-AI—may occupy important parts of this repository. They remain contents within the larger documented mind.

## How to use it

### When I need to capture something

Put it in the [Inbox](INBOX.md). Classification is optional at the moment of capture.

### When I need to find something

Use the [Index](INDEX.md), the [Mind Map](MIND-MAP.md), or follow links from a related entry.

### When something keeps returning

Add it to [Threads](THREADS.md) and connect every place where it appears.

### When something becomes structured work

Document it under [Projects](PROJECTS.md) or create whatever new area the material actually requires.

## Entry states

These labels are available when useful, not mandatory for every note:

- **Fragment** — captured before its meaning or destination is clear.
- **Memory** — an event, experience, or recollection worth retaining.
- **Observation** — something noticed or encountered.
- **Interpretation** — what something may mean.
- **Question** — something unresolved.
- **Idea** — a possibility, proposal, or mental construction.
- **Decision** — a chosen direction and its context.
- **Reference** — material kept for retrieval or connection.
- **Project** — an organized effort with continuing activity.
- **Superseded** — preserved but no longer current.

New states can be added whenever the contents require them.

## Sources and evidence

Not every entry needs an authoritative source. A thought can simply be a thought. A memory can be recorded as remembered. A question does not need to pretend it has an answer.

When an entry makes a factual, technical, historical, scientific, or otherwise verifiable claim, sources should be linked when available. When it refers to an implementation or formal artifact elsewhere, the original location should remain authoritative for that artifact.

## Public boundary

Everything committed here should be treated as public. The repository documents what I choose to expose, not every private detail that exists in my head.

## Intended result

I should be able to put anything here without first forcing it into a project-shaped box. Another person should be able to enter through any documented subject and follow the real connections outward.
